What is OpenStreetMap?

OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a global mapping project where anyone can contribute geographic data such as streets, buildings, and points of interest. The data is open and can be used in websites, apps, and research.

The map data is stored as objects with tags like highway=residential or building=yes to describe real-world features.

Why use OpenStreetMap?

  • Free and open geographic data.
  • Large and active mapping community.
  • Used in navigation apps, research, and humanitarian projects.
